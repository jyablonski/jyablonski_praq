# Covid

SARS-COV-2 is the virus first discovered in late 2019. COVID-19 is the specific disesase caused by this SARS-COV-2 virus. Coronavirus is the name for a broad family of viruses that cause respiratory infections.

COVID-19 was so deadly because:

- there wasn't a market / safety need or financial incentive to continue research with sars-cov-1 because it was contained quickly and had minimal impact, so although we had seen something similar to covid19 it didn't really help us be prepared for sars-cov-2
- it's transmissable early before we even know we're sick or infected and developed any symptoms, so it spreads very quickly
- it's a respiratory infection which is notoriously hard to fully block w/ vaccines because the virus enters through our nose or mouth
- once available, the vaccine worked to prevent severe disease and death (basically made it a milder infection), but it became clear it wasn't going to prevent transmission
- after getting the vaccine, your body still remembers some of the virus but most of your antibodies protecting against the virus fade after several months
- sars-cov-2 develops variants because it mutates as it transmits from person to person. sometimes these don't affect the virus itself, sometimes they make it more potent
- once enough people were infected, it overwhelmed healthcare systems and people who needed dire treatment couldn't all be supported.
- sars-cov-3 is possible to circulate in the future because it can develop in animals and then spill over to infect humans, unlike other viruses like smallpox or measles which only affect humans and once we're mostly all immunized it almost entirely goes away.
- if sars-cov-3 were to pop up, we'd be able to develop vaccines more quickly than we had with sars-cov-2 because the vaccine technology devleoped by pfizer and moderna is plug and play; you just put in whatever genetic sequence of the new virus' spike protein is.
- the hard part about vaccine development is safety testing, QA, logistics, clinical trials to verify effectiveness, manufacturing scale up, regulatory review etc.
