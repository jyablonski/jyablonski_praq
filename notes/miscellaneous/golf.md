# Golf

## What It Is

Target sport: hit a ball from a starting point into a hole using as few strokes as possible. Difficulty is entirely precision and self-control, not athleticism or contact.

## Course Structure

- Standard course = 18 holes, each a self-contained challenge.
- Hole length ranges from ~100 yards to 600+ yards.
- Par = strokes an expert is expected to need. Par 3 / Par 4 / Par 5.
- Full course par is usually 70–72.

### Zones of a hole

| Zone | Description |
| ------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Tee box | Starting point. Ball can be placed on a peg (tee) for the first shot only. |
| Fairway | Mown corridor toward the hole. Where you want to be. |
| Rough | Long grass flanking the fairway. Harder to control the ball. |
| Hazards | Bunkers (sand), water, trees. Water = penalty stroke. |
| Green | Ultra-short grass around the hole. Hole is 4.25" wide, marked by a flagstick. |

### Play basics

- Play the ball where it lies (mostly).
- Player farthest from the hole plays next.
- Every swing counts as a stroke — including whiffs and penalties.

## Clubs

You're allowed 14 clubs. The reason: golf requires distances from 300 yards to 3 feet, but you swing roughly the same way every time. You don't vary effort — you vary the tool.

Two levers: loft (face angle) and shaft length.

- Less loft + longer shaft = farther, lower.
- More loft = shorter, higher, softer landing.

| Club | Rough distance | Use |
| ----------------------- | -------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Driver | 230–320 yds | Tee shots on long holes. Longest, lowest loft, least forgiving. |
| Fairway woods / hybrids | 180–250 yds | Long shots off the grass; safer tee option. |
| Irons (4–9) | 130–210 yds | Workhorses. Lower number = longer + lower flight. |
| Wedges | 30–130 yds | High, steep, spinning shots into greens; bunker escapes. |
| Putter | inches–60 ft | Rolling on the green. No loft, entirely different motion. |

A set is a ladder — each club covers a ~10–15 yard rung.

## Scoring Terms

Relative to par on a single hole:

| Term | Relative to par |
| ------------------------ | --------------- |
| Albatross / Double eagle | −3 |
| Eagle | −2 |
| Birdie | −1 |
| Par | 0 |
| Bogey | +1 |
| Double bogey | +2 |

Round totals are expressed relative to course par (e.g. "−4").

### Realistic benchmarks

- Pro: ~68–72 per round. Volatile, not a grind: a typical tour round is ~4 birdies and ~3 bogeys.
- Birdies are NOT rare for pros. Eagles are (roughly 1 per 5–6 rounds, mostly on par 5s).
- Amateur: bogey golf = 90 on a par 72. That's a solid recreational score.
- Amateur skill gap is not making more pars — it's avoiding doubles and triples. Blow-up holes destroy rounds.

### Record scores

- Lowest round in a men's major: 62 — Branden Grace (2017 Open), Fowler + Schauffele (2023 U.S. Open), Schauffele + Lowry (2024 PGA). No 61 ever.
- Schauffele's 62 was 9 birdies, 9 pars, zero bogeys.
- Lowest round on tour (non-major): 58.

## Formats

### Stroke play

Sum every stroke over the round. Lowest total wins. Used by nearly all pro tournaments — 72 holes over 4 days, with a cut after 36 holes sending the bottom half home.

### Match play

Compete hole by hole. Win a hole → go "1 up." A disaster costs you that hole and nothing more, so it rewards aggression. Used by the team events.

### Stableford (points-based, higher is better)

| Result | Points |
| --------------------- | ------ |
| Double bogey or worse | 0 |
| Bogey | 1 |
| Par | 2 |
| Birdie | 3 |
| Eagle | 4 |
| Albatross | 5 |

36 points = par golf for a round.

Why it works:

1. Bad holes are auto-capped. Once you can't score a point, pick up and walk. No 12s, no death spiral, faster play.
1. Built for handicaps. Net Stableford applies handicap strokes per hole (via each hole's stroke index, 1–18 by difficulty). Lets a 22-handicap and a 6-handicap compete honestly.
1. Encourages aggression. A blowup costs at most 2 pts vs. par; a birdie gains 1.

### Modified Stableford (PGA Tour — Barracuda Championship)

| Result | Points |
| --------------------- | ------ |
| Albatross | +8 |
| Eagle | +5 |
| Birdie | +2 |
| Par | 0 |
| Bogey | −1 |
| Double bogey or worse | −3 |

Eagle worth 5, bogey costs 1 → makes "go for it" mathematically correct. Produces genuinely different strategy from stroke play.

### Other formats

- Scramble — team plays from the best ball each shot. Standard for charity/corporate events.
- Maximum Score (Rule 21.2) — committee sets a hole cap (often double/triple bogey); pick up on reaching it.

## Handicaps

A number representing how many strokes above par you typically shoot. A 15-handicap subtracts 15 strokes to compete fairly against a 5-handicap. One of the few sports with a formal mechanism for making unequal players competitive.

Net Double Bogey: under the World Handicap System, any hole you post for handicap purposes is capped at double bogey + handicap strokes received on that hole. An 11 on a par 4 gets recorded as ~6. One catastrophe shouldn't distort the picture of your ability.

## The Four Majors

All stroke play, 72 holes.

| Major | Timing | Notes |
| -------------------------------------- | ------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| The Masters | April | Augusta National, Georgia. Only major on a fixed course. Winner gets the green jacket. |
| PGA Championship | May | Rotating US courses. |
| U.S. Open | June | Brutal setups. Par is often a winning score. |
| The Open Championship ("British Open") | July | Oldest major. Windswept seaside "links" courses in the UK. Claret Jug. |

Winning all four in a career = career grand slam. Only a handful of players have done it.

## Team Events (Match Play)

| Event | Format | Cadence |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Ryder Cup | USA vs. Europe | Every 2 years. Huge atmosphere, no prize money. |
| Presidents Cup | USA vs. International (non-Europe) | Every 2 years, alternating with Ryder Cup. |
| Solheim Cup | Women's USA vs. Europe | Every 2 years. |

## Tours

- PGA Tour — primary men's US tour.
- LPGA Tour — primary women's tour.
- DP World Tour — Europe.
- LIV Golf — Saudi-backed breakaway (2022–). 54 holes, shotgun start, team format. Major source of controversy and a fractured pro landscape.
