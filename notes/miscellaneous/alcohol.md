# Alcohol

### I. The Core Logic: How Alcohol is Made

Every alcoholic drink on earth follows this linear path. You cannot have step 2 without step 1.

#### 1. Fermentation (Creation)

- The Actor: Yeast (a fungus, not a bacteria).
- The Action: Yeast eats sugar and excretes alcohol + CO2.
- The Result: A low-alcohol "wash" (like beer or wine, approx. 5–15% ABV).
- The Limit: Yeast dies if the alcohol gets too high (above ~15%), so fermentation alone cannot make hard liquor.

#### 2. Distillation (Concentration)

- The Actor: Heat (Physics).
- The Action: Boiling the fermented liquid to separate alcohol from water.
  - Alcohol boils at 173°F.
  - Water boils at 212°F.
- The Result: You capture the alcohol steam and cool it back into a liquid. This liquid is now clear and strong (approx. 40–95% ABV).
- Pot Still: A copper kettle. Inefficient but keeps the flavor (used for Cognac, Scotch).
- Column Still: An industrial tower. Efficient but strips flavor (used for Vodka).

#### 3. Aging (Refinement)

- The Actor: Time & Wood (Oak Barrels).
- The Action: The clear spirit sits in a toasted/charred wooden barrel.
- The Result:
  - Color: The clear liquid turns amber/brown.
  - Flavor: The liquid absorbs vanilla, caramel, and spice notes from the wood.
  - Smoothness: Oxygen slowly enters the barrel, removing the harsh "burn."

### II. The "Family Tree" of Alcohol

The type of alcohol is defined entirely by what sugar source you start with.

| Base Ingredient            | Fermented Only (Low ABV) | Distilled (High ABV - Clear)  | Distilled + Aged (High ABV - Brown) |
| :------------------------- | :----------------------- | :---------------------------- | :---------------------------------- |
| Grapes                     | Wine                     | Eau-de-vie / Pisco / Grappa   | Cognac / Brandy                     |
| Grains (Barley, Corn, Rye) | Beer                     | White Dog / Moonshine / Vodka | Whiskey / Scotch / Bourbon          |
| Agave (Cactus)             | Pulque                   | Silver Tequila / Mezcal       | Reposado / Añejo Tequila            |

| Rice                       | Sake                     | Soju / Shochu                 | (Rarely aged in wood)               |

### III. Key Vocabulary

#### "Dry" vs. "Sweet"

- Dry: The yeast ate all the sugar. There is zero sugar left in the liquid.
- Sweet: The fermentation was stopped early (or sugar was added later), so some sugar remains.

#### ABV vs. Proof

- ABV (Alcohol by Volume): The scientific percentage of alcohol (e.g., 40%).
- Proof: The US system, which is just double the ABV (e.g., 80 Proof).
  - _History:_ Sailors used to mix rum with gunpowder. If it was strong enough to still ignite (burn), it was "100% Proved" (Proof). This happened at 57% ABV.

#### Single Malt vs. Blend

- Single Malt: Whisky from one single distillery (e.g., Macallan). It has a very specific, unique character.
  - The vendor could be combining multiple different barrels, but they are all from the same distillery.
- Single Barrel: One Distillery + One specific Barrel. (Goal: Uniqueness/Character).
  - The vendor probably selected the best barrel from their stock to bottle separately and sell at a premium based on flavor profile etc. But, it has more variation bottle-to-bottle since it's just one barrel.
- Blend: A mix of whiskies from many different distilleries (e.g., Johnnie Walker). Designed to be smooth and consistent.
  - The vendor combines many different whiskies from different vendors / distilleries to create a unique flavor profile

#### The "Angel's Share"

- The portion of alcohol (approx. 2% per year) that evaporates through the wood of the barrel during aging. It is "lost to the angels."

#### Tannins

- Chemicals found in grape skins and wood (oak). They create that "drying" sensation in your mouth (like drinking over-steeped black tea). They act as a preservative and add structure to the drink.

### IV. Summary of Differences

- Wine/Beer: Fermented only. The flavor comes from the ingredient.
- Vodka: Distilled to be neutral. The goal is _no_ flavor.
- Gin: Vodka that is distilled again with herbs (juniper).
- Whiskey/Cognac: Distilled for concentration, then aged in wood for flavor. The goal is to balance the ingredient with the barrel.
