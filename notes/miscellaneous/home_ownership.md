## Full Monthly Payment — PITI

Taxes and insurance are based on price, not loan, so down payment does not shrink them.

| Component | Monthly | Notes |
| --------------------------- | ----------- | ----------------------------- |
| Principal & Interest (6.4%) | $4,378 | drops to $3,974 at 5.5% |
| Property tax (~1.2%) | ~$1,000 | higher with Mello-Roos |
| Insurance | ~$175 | elevated due to wildfire risk |
| **PITI total (6.4%)** | **~$5,553** | ~$5,149 at 5.5% |

HOA / Mello-Roos: $0 for many older detached homes, $100–400+/mo in newer/planned communities. Check per address.

## Home Maintenance Reserve (the spiky one)

Budget ~1% of home value per year. Spent unevenly (roof, HVAC, water heater, plumbing), but real when averaged.

| Basis | Amount |
| --------------------------- | ----------- |
| Annual reserve (~1% of $1M) | ~$10,000/yr |
| Monthly equivalent | ~$830/mo |

This is the single biggest "besides PITI" cost and the most commonly underestimated. It is NOT smooth — expect long quiet stretches then a $6K–15K hit.

## Layoff Survivability (single income, $50K fund by choice)

| Item | Value |
| ------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Survival burn (PITI + essentials, contributions paused) | ~$8,000–8,900/mo |
| Less CA unemployment (~$1,950/mo) | ~$6,000–7,000/mo net |
| $50K fund runway | ~7–8 months (if drained to zero) |
| Backstop | family help if needed |

Caveat: $50K also doubles as the buffer for a maintenance shock. A single big repair during a layoff eats into runway. Backstop noted.

## Lump-Sum Principal Paydown ($50K example)

Two paths, same lump sum, different goals:

| Path | What you request | Payment | Term | Interest saved | Fee |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------- | --------- | ---------------- | ---------------------- | --------- |
| Default (curtailment) | "apply to principal" | unchanged | ~3–4 yrs shorter | most (>$100K lifetime) | none |
| Recast | "principal payment + recast" | LOWER | same payoff date | less than default | ~$150–500 |

- Default does nothing for monthly cash flow — helps total interest only.
- Recast lowers the monthly nut — directly improves layoff survivability. Better fit for security-first priorities.
- Must explicitly tell servicer "apply to principal" or it may go to next payment / escrow.
- Confirm no prepayment penalty (rare on conventional). Conventional loans usually allow recast; FHA/VA usually don't.
- Principal paid in is illiquid — only surplus beyond the $50K cushion.

## Open Items to Verify Per Property

- HOA and/or Mello-Roos amount (can run decades)
- Whether trash is tax-covered at that address
- Solar present / addable (biggest lever on SDG&E bill)
- Exact property tax rate incl. local assessments
- Servicer's recast policy and minimum
