# RAM <> AI Q4 2025 Crisis

### 1. The Three Types of Memory

While all RAM is built from the the same raw silicon wafers, it is engineered into 3 distinct architectures based on the job it needs to do.

| Type          | System RAM (DDR5)                                                                             | Video RAM (GDDR6/7)                                                      | AI Memory (HBM3)                                                                       |
| :------------ | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Primary Goal  | Latency (Responsiveness)                                                                      | Throughput (Graphics)                                                    | Massive Volume (AI Models)                                                             |
| Analogy       | A Ferrari (fast delivery of small packages).                                                  | A Freight Train (bulk delivery of textures/pixels).                      | A Container Ship (moving entire cities of data at once).                               |
| Architecture  | Narrow Road. Uses a 64-bit bus to send small, distinct operational data to the CPU instantly. | Wide Road. Uses a 256-384 bit bus to flood the GPU with visual data.     | Superhighway. Uses a 5,120+ bit bus to keep massive AI models active in memory.        |
| Physical Form | Planar. Chips sit flat on a stick (DIMM) you plug into the motherboard.                       | Planar (Soldered). Chips sit flat, soldered permanently next to the GPU. | 3D Stacked. Chips are ground paper-thin and stacked 8-12 high directly on the GPU die. |

### 2. The Economic Crisis (Q4 2025)

In late 2025, consumer RAM prices surged by ~200-300%. This is not due to inflation, but a structural shift in manufacturing priorities known as "Wafer Displacement."

- The "Crowding Out" Effect: There are only 3 major manufacturers of memory silicon (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron). All three have pivoted their factories to prioritize Enterprise AI customers over Consumer products.
- The Profit Pivot: Enterprise clients (e.g., OpenAI, Google, Microsoft) are less price-sensitive and buy in bulk, offering significantly higher profit margins than consumer hardware.
  - Price Inelasticity: AI companies (Microsoft, OpenAI, Google) literally do not care what the price is; they need the memory now to train models. You (the consumer) care very much if RAM goes from $100 to $300.
  - The big Cloud providers are not the only players here either, smaller AI startups w/ VC funding and investment money are also competing for the same limited supply w/ a blank check trying to gain market share during this pivotal moment.
- Micron's Exit: In late 2025, major players (like Micron/Crucial) began discontinuing or severely reducing consumer-facing product lines to reallocate 100% of their resources to the AI sector.

### 3. The "Wafer War" (Why Supply is Vanishing)

The core reason for the shortage is the physical difficulty of manufacturing AI memory (HBM).

- The 3-to-1 Ratio: Manufacturing HBM is inefficient. It requires stacking fragile silicon layers and drilling thousands of microscopic holes (TSVs) through them. Industry estimates suggest that producing 1 wafer of HBM consumes the factory capacity of ~3 wafers of standard DDR5.
- Zero-Sum Game: Every factory line converted to make HBM for an AI server is a line effectively deleted from making DDR5 for home PCs.
- Conclusion: Consumers are currently paying a "scarcity tax." You are competing for the same limited raw silicon as trillion-dollar AI companies, and manufacturers are naturally prioritizing the highest bidder.

## Conclusion

This doesn't just affect games or PC builders, anything relying on RAM (laptops, phones, tablets) will see price increases as long as this shortage persists. The only foreseeable resolution is the construction of new fabrication plants dedicated to consumer memory, which could take years, or the AI bubble bursts and manufacturers reallocate capacity back to consumer products.

This is one of the many casualties of the AI boom, and its consumers who have to pay the price.

RAM is primarily affected right now, but this could potentially impact SSDs and storage as well, as the same manufacturers produce NAND flash and other memory types.

- These manufacturers are also getting a taste of what the price hikes look like. They can continue to raise their prices and claim "supply chain issues" or something else, when in reality they're just taking advantage of the AI boom because they can.
