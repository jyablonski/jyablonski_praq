Get car insurance: Before you can legally drive the car in California, you generally need to have car insurance. Contact insurance providers to get quotes and choose a policy that meets California's minimum insurance requirements.

- Do this 1-2 days before transferring title

Transfer the car title to your name: Once you have car insurance, you can proceed to transfer the car title to your name. This involves visiting the California Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) with the necessary documentation, such as the current title signed by your parents, the vehicle registration card, and possibly a completed Application for Title or Registration (Form REG 343).

Get a California driver's license: After you have the car insured and the title transferred to your name, you can apply for a California driver's license at the DMV. You may need to pass a vision exam, a written knowledge test, and possibly a driving skills test, depending on your previous driving experience and the status of your current license.

1. Double Yellow Lines
   1. A double solid yellow line indicates that there is absolutely no passing or crossing the double solid yellow line at any time to pass another vehicle, but you can cross it to make a left turn.
   2. A Broken yellow lines indicate passing can be permitted in either direction.
   3. A broken yellow line alongisde a solid yellow line meann that passing is permitted only on the side with a broken line
2. White Lines
   1. White lines separate traffic going in the same direction
   2. Broken white lines may be crossed via a lane change
   3. Solid white lines designate turn lanes and prevent lane changes near an intersection
   4. Solid white lines indicate the ridge edge of a roadway
