# Scales

### 1. The Interval Patterns

These formulas are the "DNA" of the scales. If you start on _any_ note and follow these steps, you will create that scale.
_(W = Whole Step / 2 semitones, H = Half Step / 1 semitone)_

- Major Scale Pattern: `W - W - H - W - W - W - H`
- Natural Minor Pattern: `W - H - W - W - H - W - W`

---

### 2. The 12 Major Scales

Ordered by the Circle of Fifths (adding sharps, then flats).

| Key      | Sharps/Flats | Scale Notes                              |
| :------- | :----------- | :--------------------------------------- |
| C Major  | 0            | C - D - E - F - G - A - B - C            |
| G Major  | 1 Sharp      | G - A - B - C - D - E - F# - G           |
| D Major  | 2 Sharps     | D - E - F# - G - A - B - C# - D          |
| A Major  | 3 Sharps     | A - B - C# - D - E - F# - G# - A         |
| E Major  | 4 Sharps     | E - F# - G# - A - B - C# - D# - E        |
| B Major  | 5 Sharps     | B - C# - D# - E - F# - G# - A# - B       |
| F# Major | 6 Sharps     | F# - G# - A# - B - C# - D# - E# (F) - F# |
| Db Major | 5 Flats      | Db - Eb - F - Gb - Ab - Bb - C - Db      |
| Ab Major | 4 Flats      | Ab - Bb - C - Db - Eb - F - G - Ab       |
| Eb Major | 3 Flats      | Eb - F - G - Ab - Bb - C - D - Eb        |
| Bb Major | 2 Flats      | Bb - C - D - Eb - F - G - A - Bb         |
| F Major  | 1 Flat       | F - G - A - Bb - C - D - E - F           |

> Note on Enharmonics: F# Major (6 sharps) and Gb Major (6 flats) sound exactly the same on the piano. F# is listed above, but Gb Major is: `Gb - Ab - Bb - Cb - Db - Eb - F - Gb`.

---

### 3. The 12 Natural Minor Scales

These use the minor interval pattern (`W-H-W...`). They correspond to the Major keys directly above them (Relative Minors).

| Key      | Relative Major | Scale Notes                              |
| :------- | :------------- | :--------------------------------------- |
| A Minor  | C              | A - B - C - D - E - F - G - A            |
| E Minor  | G              | E - F# - G - A - B - C - D - E           |
| B Minor  | D              | B - C# - D - E - F# - G - A - B          |
| F# Minor | A              | F# - G# - A - B - C# - D - E - F#        |
| C# Minor | E              | C# - D# - E - F# - G# - A - B - C#       |
| G# Minor | B              | G# - A# - B - C# - D# - E - F# - G#      |
| D# Minor | F#             | D# - E# (F) - F# - G# - A# - B - C# - D# |
| Bb Minor | Db             | Bb - C - Db - Eb - F - Gb - Ab - Bb      |
| F Minor  | Ab             | F - G - Ab - Bb - C - Db - Eb - F        |
| C Minor  | Eb             | C - D - Eb - F - G - Ab - Bb - C         |
| G Minor  | Bb             | G - A - Bb - C - D - Eb - F - G          |
| D Minor  | F              | D - E - F - G - A - Bb - C - D           |

### A Note on "Other" Minor Scales

While the lists above are the Natural Minors (the most common), you might occasionally encounter two variations used to make melodies sound smoother or harmonies stronger:

1.  Harmonic Minor: Raise the 7th note of the Natural Minor by one half-step. (e.g., in A Minor, G becomes G#).
2.  Melodic Minor: Raise both the 6th and 7th notes when going _up_, but revert to natural minor when going _down_.
